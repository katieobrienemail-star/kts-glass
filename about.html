Artist Statement

I'm aiming for my work to sit at a meeting point of art and science. With a background in Fine Art (BA(Hons) UAL) and ongoing studies in Psychology and Neuroscience, recently I use stained glass to explore how our minds shape our experience.

I’m particularly interested in awe—an emotion now recognised and studied as a distinct emotion in psychology, often provoked when we encounter something vast, beautiful, or beyond comprehension. Stained glass is a natural catalyst for this: when light floods a space with colour and transforms it, a natural reminder that something bigger than ourselves exists.

I also love creating fun stained glass panels for people to enjoy in their homes. Cats, in particular, are a recurring and joyful theme in my work.

About Me

I have a BA(Hons) in Fine Art Painting from UAL and am currently studying for a BSc in Psychology, driven by my fascination with neuroscience and the ways creativity and science are intwined.

I've completed a Level 2 Glass course at Morley College with Maria Zuletta, earning a Distinction, and have continued to develop my practice through glass painting, printing, and etching techniques. I've also experimented with casting, glass blowing and cold working. Recently I've aimed for my work to connect directly with psychology and neuroscience, combining concept with traditional craft.

One example is my stained-glass panel inspired by Penfield’s homunculus—a visual map of how the brain “sees” the body. Here, the hands, lips, and tongue appear huge, not because they’re bigger in real life, but because the brain gives them more attention for touch and movement. Blending this scientific model with Baroque-inspired colour and dramatic light, I wanted to portray the brain as a place of both perception and wonder.

Alongside my studio practice, I volunteer at the Natural History Museum, where I help children learn about science through crafts. This reflects my wider goal as an artist: to make complex ideas about the mind and the body not only understandable, but also exciting, playful, and awe-inspiring.

Recently I've been focusing on using stained glass as a bridge between art and science, inspiring curiosity and offering moments of wonder. However, I also love creating fun stained glass panels for people to enjoy in their homes. Cats, in particular, are a recurring and joyful theme in my work.
